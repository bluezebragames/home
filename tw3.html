A brief description of each book

Book 1:

In That Which 1, also known as Twitch 1, a lot of setup is done. The Ptrocanfer is introduced, along with Danté and Theódore, forming the trio. Other recurring characters such as Don Samuel and Sensei Juan Voodoo, Ph.D. are mentioned briefly. Future plot elements such as everlasting everlast, a drink which addicts instantly, The Ptrocanfer's gun/phone, a device which becomes the opposite of what The Ptrocanfer wants, and The Ptrocanfer's nose, which grew suddenly on his foot when he ate okra.

Book 2:

In Twitch 2, action starts happening. We often joke that we can write at most one half of a book which is good. Almost never is this more true than in 2. The first half is supposed to be about a magical cooler which is actually a timetravel machine. It really wasn't that good, though. The second half of 2 is widely considered to be a dramatic improvement as it changes the focus to one of the best sections of Twitch ever: The Bo0lean Chronicles. Bo0lean is a detective who also happens to be Danté, but only after Danté "dies." He gets his first case when a man by the name of N.C. Radovan loses his hat and comes to Bo0lean a year later. The only problem is that everyone in the book is severely incompetent, especially N.C. Radovan. After the Bo0lean Chronicles is the adventures of Don Samuel, who became "Feline Dude" after he ate a mouse. His superpower is the ability to just keep dying over and over again, and it is used to full extent, dying in basically every chapter, sometimes even multiple times per chapter. In any case, the book ends with the mediocre "Lord of the Socks," a Lord of the Rings parody which really never took off.
