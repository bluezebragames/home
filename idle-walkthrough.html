Our Idle Game:

An Overview

Our idle game is intended to parody both traditional idle games and also the book-writing process.

To begin the game a player clicks on a sheet of paper, and each click increases the number of units (initially words) a player has by one.  Unit is defined as the item generated by a click.  The unit is initially a word, and progresses as follows:

  • word
  • sentence
  • paragraph
  • chapter
  • novel
  • book series.

A player moves up the hierarchy of units by purchasing an upgrade using gold.  Each unit is worth a set amount of gold.  The best strategy for a player is to upgrade as click as possible because a quick generates a unit, and as the hierarchy progresses, each successive unit is more valuable.  As a player clicks, his quantity of gold will increase according to the following valuation:

  • word (1 gold)
  • sentence (5 gold)
  • paragraph (25 gold)
  • chapter (125 gold)
  • novel (625 gold)
  • book series (3125 gold).

The game includes upgrades.  Contemplated upgrades to clicking power in the game include:

  • a thesaurus
  • a typing course
  • optometrist visit
  • voice to text software package.

Contemplated upgrades to buildings include:

  • monkey training
  • bananas
  • ergonomic keyboard.

Another important element of the game is that of buildings.  These generate units whenever a player is actively playing the game.  Players may build buildings.  Contemplated buildings in the game include:

  • monkeys
  • computers.

As a player purchases buildings, they become increasingly expensive.  Progression in the game will bring a player to the point of hitting bottlenecks.  Bottlenecks occur as players are about to transition to the next phase of the game.

  • Phase 1: player works through the units, writing books.
  • Phase 2: book quality and marketing generate increased book sales.
  • Phase 3: translation into all languages, in reverse order of popularity.

Production eventually reaches a cap, because the player is missing what is provided in the next stage.  To transition to a subsequent phase, the player will need to save up  (not purchase buildings or upgrades) a significant amount of gold.
